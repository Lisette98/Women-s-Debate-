About

The Woman’s Debate (“TWD”) was created in 2015 by a team of leaders in the fields of traditional and social media, corporate America, entrepreneurial organizations, and non-profit associations. TWD aims to reduce gender inequality by deploying a presidential debate to discuss issues specifically affecting women in America. TWD hopes that by elevating these issues and introducing them into the national presidential debate, it can help to raise awareness and begin to reduce gender inequality.

TWD is seeking input to enable it to convene questions for our political candidates— from experts in the areas of violence against women, workplace discrimination, healthcare, etc. This initiative will generate solutions proposed by these candidates to advance women in four areas: safe and just recourse for women when it comes to violence in the home, in the military, in prisons and on campus; reduction of gender discrimination in the workplace, accommodation for pregnant and working mothers, and support in health care opportunities that ensure marginalized female populations are taken care of.

Rather than duplicate the efforts of leaders and organizations that serve women today, TWD will facilitate a common platform among existing organizations, whether for-profits, nonprofits, NGOs, and academic institutions working to encourage the government to effect change. No one organization can do this by itself.

Mission

The Women’s Debate serves as a platform to convene a collective voice and facilitate questions for our political candidates.  This platform will generate data-driven solutions proposed by these candidates to advance women in four areas: safe and just recourse for women when it comes to violence in the home, in the military, in prisons and on campus; reduction of gender discrimination in the workplace, accommodation for pregnant and working mothers, and support in health care opportunities that ensure marginalized female populations are taken care of.

Who We Are



Johanna Zeilstra

Johanna Zeilstra is an established business strategist and an accomplished leader for both global corporations and start-ups. Over the past two decades, she has worked at PricewaterhouseCoopers and JPMorgan, and with over a dozen Fortune 500 clients. Her career has led her to work in Canada, China, Indonesia, France, The Netherlands, Mexico and across the USA. She acts as a coach to senior executives on boards and top teams, as well as runs a women entrepreneurs network in New York and Connecticut. She lives in Westchester with her husband and three sons.


David S. Kidder

David S. Kidder is an entrepreneur with a wide range of marketing and technology expertise focused on social, mobile, and search advertising and product development. David is the co-founder of Bionic, an enterprise accelerator platform. Prior to Bionic, David served as the co-founder and CEO of Clickable, an online advertising software as a service (acquired by Syncapse). Prior to Clickable, Kidder co-founded SmartRay Network, a mobile advertising pioneer (acquired by LifeMinders), and Net-X, a web-authoring platform (acquired by Target Vision). Kidder is a graduate of the Rochester Institute of Technology, and received ID Magazine's International Design Award and Ernst and Young's Entrepreneur of the Year Award in 2008. David is an active angel investor through his fund, Alt Option Return, is a founding member of The Acumen Fund One Percent Fund, and serves on the National Board of the Smithsonian. He is the creator and co-author of the two-time New York Times bestselling five book series, The Intellectual Devotional, published by Rodale. His latest book The Startup Playbook, was released in January 2013, published by Chronicle Books. 


Joy Tutela

Joy Tutela is a literary agent who specializes in books on the subject of women's and LGBT issues as well as workplace, health and parenting topics. A graduate of Wellesley College and the Radcliffe Publishing Program, she has been writing instructor for Goucher College, worked for Harvard Business School Press and was an elementary school teacher in Italy.
